Efficient Multiplier Architectures for CNN-Based Image Processor

Focusing on Modified Booth Encoding (MBE) and 4-to-2 compressor-based hardware acceleration for high-performance AI implementations.

Abstract

The Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) achieves high accuracy and is widely used in image-related applications. Recent evolution of CNN-based AI applications has made their hardware implementation a challenging task. Efficient CNN accelerator architectures require optimization in processing elements (PEs), especially the multiplier and adder units. This research presents strategies to optimize the multipliers and typical adder trees, improving CNN system performance, reducing hardware resource usage, and enhancing power efficiency.

Introduction

Hardware customization for CNN accelerators remains a challenging problem due to the bulky nature of MAC components and delays in conventional adder trees. The proposed work designs a Modified Booth Encoding (MBE) multiplier to replace conventional MAC units on FPGA, later intended for ASIC implementation. 4-to-2 compressors are used instead of typical adder trees to reduce critical path delay, significantly lowering hardware consumption while maintaining computational throughput.

Methodology

  • Design of Modified Booth Encoding (MBE) multipliers to efficiently generate partial products.
  • Replacement of conventional adder trees with 4-to-2 compressor-based summation to reduce delays.
  • Simulation and verification of RTL for FPGA deployment.
  • Evaluation of area, power, and performance against state-of-the-art implementations.
  • Integration into CNN accelerator hardware for image processing applications.

Results & Benefits

  • Significant reduction in hardware resource consumption.
  • Faster execution of convolution operations in the PE unit.
  • Power-efficient multiplier architecture suitable for AI image processors.
  • Flexible for FPGA prototyping and later ASIC deployment.
