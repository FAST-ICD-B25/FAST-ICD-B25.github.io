Design of a 4-Bit AC Nano-Processor

For Self-Powered Biomedical and Smart Dust Applications

Abstract

The development of a 4-bit AC nano-processor represents a significant shift in the design of self-powered IoT nodes and "smart dust." Traditional wireless power transfer systems are limited by the inefficiency of RF-to-DC rectifiers and the large storage capacitors required. The AC-logic family utilizes the signal phase to represent logic states (0° = High, 180° = Low), while QRF-only logic leverages quadrature signals (I & Q) for timing control such as pre-charge, hold, and evaluate. This rectifier-less architecture removes the most power-hungry components of conventional designs, enabling ultra-low power operation.

Introduction

Experimental validation across different CMOS nodes demonstrates the suitability of AC and QRF logic families for complex processing. In 65 nm CMOS, fundamental AC gates (NOT, NAND, NOR) consume between 8.2 nW and 20.8 nW. Integrating these components into a 4-bit nano-processor provides a framework for implantable biomedical devices and autonomous sensors, deriving power and data synchronization directly from transmitted RF fields. This simplifies single-chip wirelessly powered applications and ensures long-term operation in battery-free environments.

Methodology

  • Design and simulation of AC and QRF logic cells for low-power operations.
  • Integration of AC logic gates into a 4-bit nano-processor schematic.
  • Experimental validation of fundamental gates across CMOS nodes.
  • Optimization of phase-domain signal propagation for reliable processing.
  • Preparation for layout refinement and eventual tapeout.

Results & Benefits

  • Ultra-low power consumption of 8.2–20.8 nW per gate in 65 nm CMOS.
  • Battery-free operation using RF-derived power and timing.
  • Feasibility for implantable biomedical devices and smart dust applications.
  • Single-chip solution simplifies hardware and ensures long-term reliability.
  • Next steps: schematic refinement and tapeout preparation.
