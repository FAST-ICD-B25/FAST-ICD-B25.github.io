Design of an Integrated Modulo Sampling ADC

For SRDR and Passive Radar Applications

Abstract

Analog-to-digital converters (ADCs) are essential for converting real-world analog signals into digital data. A critical parameter is the dynamic range (DR), which determines the ratio between the largest and smallest signals the ADC can resolve. Signals exceeding the DR cause clipping and degrade the quality of digitized signals. This project implements a **modulo-based folding architecture** to improve ADC dynamic range, enabling high-fidelity digitization even for large amplitude variations, useful for passive radar, software-defined radios, and wideband multi-standard receivers.

Introduction

The proposed **Modulo Sampling ADC** folds signals exceeding the ADC's dynamic range back into the valid range before sampling. Using a feedback mechanism, a trigger activates a voltage generator to correct the input signal dynamically, avoiding saturation. This allows the ADC to digitize high-dynamic-range signals as low-dynamic-range signals, preserving fidelity and enabling robust operation in wideband receivers and passive radar systems.

Methodology

  • Design an integrated modulo ADC with folding architecture.
  • Implement trigger circuitry to detect signals beyond ADC dynamic range.
  • Use direct voltage feedback to bring signals back within range.
  • Sample the folded low-dynamic-range signal using standard ADC cores.
  • Validate design through simulation and prototype hardware evaluation.

Results & Benefits

  • Improved ADC dynamic range without increasing hardware complexity.
  • Prevention of signal clipping for high-amplitude analog inputs.
  • Applicability in wideband passive radar and SRDR systems.
  • High-fidelity digitization for software-defined radio and biomedical instrumentation.
  • Scalable architecture for integration into next-generation ADC designs.
